ABOUT RATH YATRA

Rath Yatra, also known as the Chariot Festival, is a major Hindu festival dedicated to Lord Jagannath, an avatar of Lord Vishnu. It is celebrated annually in Puri, Odisha, and other places, where grand processions feature chariots carrying the deities Jagannath, Balabhadra, and Subhadra. The festival represents the journey of the deities from their temple to their aunt's house â€” the Gundicha Temple.

Key Aspects of Rath Yatra:

  • Celebration: Millions of devotees participate, pulling the chariots along designated routes with joy and devotion.
  • Deities: Lord Jagannath, Lord Balabhadra, and Goddess Subhadra are carried in beautifully decorated chariots.
  • Chariots: Massive wooden structures built anew each year by expert carpenters known as "Maharana".
  • Significance: Symbolizes divine journey, unity, and love for the deities. It marks their trip to the Gundicha Temple.
  • Rituals: Includes Mangalarpana, Chhera Panhara (where the King sweeps the chariots), and the pulling of chariots.
  • Global Celebration: ISKCON spreads the tradition globally with Rath Yatras held in major cities worldwide.
  • Puri Rath Yatra: The most famous one, drawing pilgrims from across the world.
